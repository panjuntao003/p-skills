# P Invest Rules

These rules keep the system disciplined without turning it into direct financial advice.

## Core Principle

Good company, good price, good patience.

Do not collapse those into one sentence. A good company can be a bad investment at the wrong price or in the wrong personal context.

## Source Discipline

Every external fact in a formal artifact should include:

```text
Source | Confidence A/B/C/D/E | Publication date or access date
```

Confidence guide:

- A: official disclosure, filing, regulator, exchange, central bank, statistics bureau
- B: reputable data vendor or institution
- C: professional media or reputable analyst source
- D: general media, useful only as a lead
- E: social media or anonymous claim, not enough for formal conclusions

Use A/B/C for formal analysis. Use D/E only to discover questions to verify.

## Freshness

Refresh or mark stale:

- Prices and market data: same day
- News and events: event window
- Institution views: 7 days
- Macro monthly data: next release cycle
- Company financials: latest quarter or annual report
- Company thesis: whenever a major filing, policy, management, or competitive event occurs

## Four Checks Before Action

Before a buy, add, sell, or reduce evaluation, check:

1. Environment: is broad risk acceptable?
2. Business: is this a good and understandable company?
3. Price: what is the valuation anchor?
4. Psychology: is the user reacting to FOMO, fear, anchoring, or social proof?

## Decision Guardrails

- Do not invent execution price, quantity, or user intent.
- If the user reports a completed trade, record it faithfully and add a discipline note if process was skipped.
- Do not sell only because the price fell.
- Do not buy only because a target price was reached.
- Always write the failure condition: what evidence would prove the thesis wrong?

## Review Discipline

Review decisions to improve judgment, not to prove intelligence.

Classify mistakes as:

- bad data
- misunderstanding
- emotional decision
- valuation error
- catalyst error
- ignored risk
- process violation
- luck mistaken for skill

Turn repeated mistakes into rule changes.
