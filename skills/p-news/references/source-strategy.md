# Source Strategy

This skill borrows `builder-digest`'s core taste: track people and teams that build products and publish original views, not accounts that only relay others' work.

## Keep

- Company/lab release notes, research blogs, docs, model cards, changelogs, and official repos.
- Builder posts with concrete product, architecture, eval, distribution, or failure details.
- Research papers when they imply a near-term model, agent, infrastructure, or product pattern.
- Podcasts/interviews when the guest is a founder, researcher, engineer, or operator with first-hand information.
- Chinese AI builder sources that reveal local product adoption, workflow, distribution, or regulation differences.

## Downrank

- Generic "top tools" or listicle posts.
- Rewritten news with no original link.
- Speculation without a named source.
- Thin social commentary that does not change a product, model, company, or builder thesis.
- Duplicates across aggregators.

## Scoring Heuristic

Score each candidate 0-3 on:

- Freshness: did this happen inside the requested window?
- Originality: is there a primary source or first-hand builder claim?
- Practicality: can a builder, researcher, investor, or operator act on this?
- Durability: will this still matter next week?
- Novelty: does it change what the user should watch?

Keep items scoring 10+ unless the user requested exhaustive coverage. For broad daily briefs, target 5-12 items.

## Builder Signal Section

Use this section for:

- People/projects worth adding to the source graph
- Repeated appearances across independent sources
- New repos or products that deserve a later technical evaluation
- Disagreements between credible builders

Write the signal as a concrete follow-up, for example:

- "Watch the repo for evals and issue velocity."
- "Track founder posts for enterprise adoption proof."
- "Compare this claim with official benchmark or model card."
